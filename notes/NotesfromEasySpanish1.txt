Chapter 1: Nouns, Articles, and Adjectives
A noun is a person, place, thing. In Spanish, all nouns are MASCULINE or FEMININE.
In spanish the definite article (english The) agrees with the noun's gender and number:
el, la, los, las

Nouns ending in -ista or -nte can be Masc. or Fem. The article indicates gender.
el artista	the (male) artist
la artista	the (female) artist

el pianista	the (male) artist
la pianista	the (female) artist

el cantante	the (male) singer
la cantante	the (female) singer

el presidente	the (male) president
la presidente	the (female) president

FOR MASCULINE NOUNS THAT END IN VOWEL - Add an s. el becomes los.
el día - los días

If it ends in an L - Add an es.
el animal	los animales


FOR FEMININE NOUNS THAT END IN VOWEL - Add an s. la becomes las.
la bolsa	las bolsas

If it ends in a consonant - Add es.
la canción	las canciónes
la mujer	las mujeres

Indefinite Article (inglés a, an)

un for masc. una for fem.

un amigo	a (male) friend
un baño		a bathroom
un espejo	a mirror

un museo	a museum
un sillón	an armchair
un tiquete	a ticket

una amiga	a female friend
una biblioteca	a library
una ciudad	a city
una librería	a bookstore	-a "librar-i-a" is a bookstore
una maleta	a suitcase
una mujer	a woman
una página	a page
una persona	a person (male or female)
una pluma	a pen

NOTICE: el mapa -> un mapa

The plural indefinite article (inglés "some") is unos, unas

unos barcos	some boats	-Boats don't bark
unas casas	some houses

Adjectives follow the noun in a sentence and agree in gender/number.

el libro blanco		the white book

el hombre hermoso	the handsome man

la lámpara roja		the red lamp
la mujer hermosa	the beautiful woman
