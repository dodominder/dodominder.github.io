A simple trick to comprehending Celsius

Being an American and never having to use Celsius in everyday life I often feel like a rube when I see a temperature listed in it. What does 20 °C feel like?

There’s a formula of course but it’s too complicated to rapidly use in your head. I just want a ballpark idea of what °C feels like.

So here’s a rough and ready method for a range of temperatures that I encounter from weather.

°C °F rounded °F
-10 14 10
0 32 30
10 50 50
20 68 70
30 86 90

A pattern quickly emerged after making this table! Starting at -10 °C and increasing by 10 the rounded °F increments by 20.

It’s easy to remember. 0 is the freezing point of water in degrees Celsius and 32 for Fahrenheit. Round 32 to 30 and you can remember 0 and 30 as starting points.

Celsius increments or decrements by 10 and Fahrenheit by 20.
So it’s 25 °C outside? That feels like 80 °F.

8 °C ? A bit below 50 °F.

So if someone says “Il fait trente trois degrés dehors !”
Say to yourself “Okay. 30. So that’s 3 increments from 0. And 3 times 20 plus 30 equals 90. So it’s around 90 °F.”
